## What Freedom Beagle is

Freedom Beagle is an independent journal on constitutional rights, the law as it actually applies to you, and the current events testing both. Its subject, underneath every story, is a single question: what is *truly American* — the principles the country was built on — and how do we find our way back to them?

A beagle is a scent hound. It exists to find a trail that went cold and follow it home. That is the whole idea.

## No party. No masters. No leash.

Freedom Beagle takes no side between the two major political parties. It endorses no candidates and extends no benefit of the doubt to anyone holding power, in either party, regardless of the letter after their name.

Our starting premise is on the record and older than the parties themselves: political parties appear nowhere in the Constitution, and the men who wrote that document warned against them. What we have instead is two wings on the same eagle, beating in opposite directions — and the bird rarely flies where the people want it to go. When money moves where it shouldn't, when a right shrinks while no one is watching, or when a scandal is quietly buried, we consider it worth following, and we do not first check which party it embarrasses.

## Our editorial standards

These are not aspirations. They are the rules every piece on this site is written to follow, and you are entitled to hold us to them.

1. **Sourced method.** Every factual claim links to a document, ruling, filing, dataset, or audit log. We will say a thing plainly and sometimes sharply — but we show our work, every time. A claim without a receipt does not run.

2. **Facts, not advice.** We report what the law says, what courts have ruled, and what named organizations recommend — always attributed. We do not tell you what to do, and we do not give legal advice. Our pieces about your rights report on rulings; they are not instructions, and they are not a substitute for a lawyer.

3. **Formal citations.** Legal claims carry their formal reference in the text — case reporter citations (e.g., *Riley v. California*, 573 U.S. 373 (2014)), statute sections (e.g., 18 U.S.C. § 1001), and constitutional provisions (e.g., U.S. Const. amend. IV) — with full, linked citations listed at the foot of every legal piece.

4. **Defined terms.** Every legal term of art is defined as it is defined in Black's Law Dictionary, with the edition cited, so no reader has to guess whether a word carries its legal meaning or its everyday one.

5. **Quantified and balanced.** Where a story turns on numbers, we give the numbers, cited, and prefer neutral or official sources (courts, the Congressional Research Service, government data) for the hard figures; when we cite an advocacy organization, we say so and note where its methodology is contested. Every investigative and audit piece states the strongest version of the opposing case, in its own right, before we close.

## Corrections policy

We will make mistakes. When a journal's entire value rests on being right, the honest response to error is not to hide it — it is to fix it in the open.

- **When we learn of a material error, we correct it promptly** and note the correction, with its date, at the bottom of the affected article.
- **Substantive corrections are also logged below,** permanently, so the record of what we got wrong and when we fixed it is public.
- **Figures drawn from live, crowdsourced, or point-in-time datasets** (for example, camera counts from DeFlock) are labeled with the date they were retrieved. These numbers change over time; a later, different figure is an update, not necessarily a correction.
- **A public channel for reporting errors is being established** and will be posted here once it is live. We would rather show no contact address than publish one that does not yet work — the same standard we hold our reporting to.

## Independence and funding

Freedom Beagle is independently owned and operated. It carries no advertising, answers to no political party, campaign, corporation, or donor, and has no outside owner directing its coverage. It is not funded by any organization named in its reporting. If that ever changes — if the journal takes on funding of any kind — this section will disclose it before a single dollar shapes a story.

## Bylines

Freedom Beagle publishes under a unified masthead. Reported pieces carry the byline "Freedom Beagle Staff," reflecting that the journal speaks with one editorial voice and that one person stands behind every word as founder and editor, accountable for the standards above. This is a deliberate convention, not a claim to a large newsroom.
