*The Grievance Audit has held the modern federal record against the twenty-seven grievances of the Declaration of Independence, one entry at a time. This is the last. It covers the five war grievances — and then does the thing a fact-checking journal owes its readers at the end: it grades honestly, including grading the Declaration itself. Terms per Black's Law Dictionary. Reporting on documents, not legal advice.*

## The grievances

> "He has abdicated Government here, by declaring us out of his Protection and waging War against us."
> "He has plundered our seas, ravaged our Coasts, burnt our towns, and destroyed the lives of our people."
> "He is at this time transporting large Armies of foreign Mercenaries to compleat the works of death, desolation and tyranny..."
> "He has constrained our fellow Citizens taken Captive on the high Seas to bear Arms against their Country..."
> "He has excited domestic insurrections amongst us, and has endeavoured to bring on the inhabitants of our frontiers, the merciless Indian Savages, whose known rule of warfare, is an undistinguished destruction of all ages, sexes and conditions."
> — Declaration of Independence (1776)

## The honest finding: these describe a war, and a war is not our subject

The first four of these are not grievances about governance. They are a war correspondent's dispatch: towns burned, coasts ravaged, Hessian mercenaries shipped in, captured sailors forced to fire on their own. They describe a monarch who had stopped governing his subjects and started killing them.

A fact-checking journal earns its credibility as much by the parallels it **refuses** to draw as by the ones it does. There is no honest modern equivalent to a government burning its own cities and hiring foreign armies to slaughter its citizens. To manufacture one — to strain some domestic dispute into "burnt our towns" — would be exactly the dishonesty this series exists to avoid. So we say it plainly: **these grievances describe the end of governance and the beginning of war, and the modern United States does not do this to its people.** That is not a small thing to be able to write. It is, quietly, the most important line in the whole audit.

**One faint, honest echo — and only one.** The concern about "foreign Mercenaries" has a modern cousin: the American military's heavy reliance on private contractors, who at points in the Iraq and Afghanistan wars rivaled or outnumbered uniformed troops, according to Congressional Research Service and Congressional Budget Office analyses. It is a real phenomenon worth watching. But it is deployed in America's wars *abroad*, not against Americans at home, and honesty requires keeping the echo in proportion: it is a footnote to this grievance, not a fulfillment of it.

## The line that shames the page

And now the hardest paragraph in this series, because integrity does not get to stop at the government's door.

The final grievance calls Native Americans "merciless Indian Savages" whose "known rule of warfare, is an undistinguished destruction of all ages, sexes and conditions." It is a racist libel, written into the founding document of a nation that in the same breath declared all men created equal. It was false when it was written, and it helped license two centuries of dispossession and slaughter aimed at the people it slandered.

This series has spent thirteen entries holding the modern government to the standard of 1776. Honesty — the whole premise of this journal — demands that the standard cut in every direction, including backward. The men who wrote the Declaration were not gods. Many of them enslaved human beings. One of the charges in their great indictment of tyranny was itself an act of the very contempt for human dignity they were claiming to oppose. To find our way back to what is *truly* American, we have to be able to say both things at once: the Declaration is one of the most liberating documents ever written, **and** it contains a line that is a permanent stain. A country that can only worship its founding cannot learn from it. Reverence that cannot survive an honest reading is not reverence. It is fear.

## The scorecard: 27 grievances, 2026

Here is the whole audit, netted out. No party's talking points, just where the record lands.

**Answered — founding successes the republic kept:**
- No standing-army coup; civilian control of the military holds ([No. 4](/freedom-beagle/articles/grievance-audit-04-military-above-civil-power.html))
- Soldiers are not quartered in homes ([No. 5](/freedom-beagle/articles/grievance-audit-05-quartering-troops.html))
- Congress cannot be dissolved; elections cannot be canceled ([No. 9](/freedom-beagle/articles/grievance-audit-09-representative-government.html))
- Article III judges have real, durable independence ([No. 10](/freedom-beagle/articles/grievance-audit-10-judges-dependent.html))
- States govern themselves; their charters are secure ([No. 13](/freedom-beagle/articles/grievance-audit-13-charters-and-governments.html))
- The government does not wage war on its own people (this entry)

**Ringing true today — grievances the record echoes:**
- The jury trial is nearly extinct; forfeiture and agency courts route around it ([No. 1](/freedom-beagle/articles/grievance-audit-01-trial-by-jury.html))
- An administrative state so large the government cannot count its own agencies ([No. 2](/freedom-beagle/articles/grievance-audit-02-swarms-of-officers.html))
- Qualified immunity shields officials from the liability Congress wrote into law ([No. 6](/freedom-beagle/articles/grievance-audit-06-mock-trial-immunity.html))
- Four million Americans taxed without a vote ([No. 7](/freedom-beagle/articles/grievance-audit-07-taxes-without-consent.html))
- Immigration judges dependent on the executive that appears before them ([No. 10](/freedom-beagle/articles/grievance-audit-10-judges-dependent.html))

**Contested — the live fights, checked but strained in 2025–26:**
- Troops in American cities, blocked by the courts ([No. 3](/freedom-beagle/articles/grievance-audit-03-standing-armies.html))
- Trade taxed by executive decree, partly reversed by the Court ([No. 8](/freedom-beagle/articles/grievance-audit-08-cutting-off-trade.html))
- People flown to a foreign prison, the courts fighting to still reach them ([No. 11](/freedom-beagle/articles/grievance-audit-11-transported-beyond-seas.html))
- Gerrymandered maps the courts stepped away from policing ([No. 9](/freedom-beagle/articles/grievance-audit-09-representative-government.html))

**The honest verdict:** neither "everything is fine" nor "everything is tyranny." The republic answered the grievances that were about brute force and kept those answers. It is losing ground on the quieter ones — the slow, procedural, bureaucratic erosions that don't burn a town but hollow a right. Tyranny in 1776 arrived by ship and musket. In 2026 it would arrive, if it arrives, by docket number, backlog, and delegation — which is precisely why it is harder to see, and why counting it, out loud and with citations, is the entire point of this journal.

## The audit

The last grievances describe a war that ended in 1783, and the honest reader lets them stay there. The Declaration that recorded them is a masterpiece with a stain, and the honest reader holds both. And the country those grievances founded has kept its promises against open force while straining against its own paperwork — which is the finding twenty-seven entries were always going to reach, if the counting was honest.

*Historia magistra vitae* — history is the teacher of life. We audited the teacher's own indictment against her most famous student. The grades are mixed, the record is public, and the assignment — finding our way back to what is truly American — was never going to be finished by a journal. Only by the people it reports for.

*This concludes The Grievance Audit. The scent is never fully cold; when the record moves — a ruling, a filing, a number — the series will move with it.*
